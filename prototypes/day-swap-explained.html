The summary in one screen

Today's "Change day plan" link is pink, loud, and repeated on every day row. Worse, it's the only entry point. The fix: 3 entry points (one for each way users look at a day), but each one quieter than today's link.

What changes

  • Replace the loud pink "Change day plan" link with a small grey "Change day" pill on the right of each day title.
  • Add the same pill on the day detail page (when they tap a day to read it deeply).
  • Add a floating pill in the full-screen video player (when they're watching a day's videos). This is the killer entry.
  • Tap = popup with the current day pinned + 2 or 3 alternative day variants named with editorial titles like "Up before dawn" or "Pulse of the jungle".
  • If swapping creates 3 high-energy days in a row, show a gentle one-line warning: "Makes Days 1-3 all active."
  • Reorder days by drag handle, but only within the same city or region. Days that move you between cities are tagged "Transfer" and can't be shuffled.
  • We do not let users edit individual activities. That was a product decision: too much cognitive load, harder to curate.

Context

Why day swap is harder than hotel swap

Hotels are simple: one card, one brand name, one price. Days are bundles of activities with a vibe. That makes day swap harder in three ways:

  • A day is a vibe, not a brand. "Adventure" or "Wellness", not "Hilton" or "Marriott". We have to name the alternatives ourselves.
  • The user sees a day in three different places. The at-a-glance list, the day detail page, and the full-screen video viewer. Each place is a different mood. The swap entry should follow them.
  • Knock-on effects. Changing Day 1 to "Adventure" might make Day 1-2-3 all be high-energy back-to-back, which is exhausting. Hotel swaps don't have this problem.
User psychology

The dominant feeling: tired but wanting it to feel like "me"

By the time the user reaches day plans, they've already chosen destination, dates, hotels. They're tired. They want:

  • Identity match. "Are we ATV people or yoga people? Make this trip feel like us."
  • Pace control. "Day 2 looks packed. Give me something slower."
  • Reassurance. Most users skim and don't swap. They just want to know they could.

Frequency ranking:

  1. 50-60% · Read and reassure, no swap.
  2. 20-25% · Adjust the pace of one or two days.
  3. 10-15% · Swap the theme (beach vs temple).
  4. ~5% · Reorder days. Out of scope (see Q5).
😴

"Make it mine, fast"

Decision fatigue is real. Three taps max.

Key insight: the goal isn't to let them rebuild the trip. It's to let them nudge it 10-20% toward their self-image so they sign off feeling ownership.

Recommendation: A + C + D together.

The user looks at a day in three different moods, and each mood has its own entry:

  • Scanning (the at-a-glance list) → small grey pill on each row.
  • Studying (the day detail page) → outlined pill in the header, next to Photos and Videos.
  • Experiencing (full-screen videos) → quiet floating pill at the bottom.

📖Studying: outlined pill in the header
🎬Experiencing: floating pill on the video

This is how Instagram's "Save" button appears on the grid, on the post, and on the reel - same icon, three places.

Why we reject the global "Edit days" button (B): users always edit one day at a time, never all of them together. So "Edit mode" buys nothing. And if the entry is in the section header, it disappears as soon as the user scrolls down to Day 3.
Why we reject the caption-tap entry (E): there's no visual hint that captions are tappable. The user will never discover it. Instead, tapping the caption (or the day name in the video viewer) should open the day detail page (option C), which is the recognisable Instagram pattern.
Question 2

2How do we name the alternatives?

For hotels, the alternative is the brand name ("Komaneka at Bisma"). For days, there's no brand. We have to name them.

You called out that repeating the city name ("Adventure Ubud") is redundant since the user already knows which city they're in. Agreed. Naming options:

  • "Day 1 Option B". Generic. No emotion.
  • Theme + city: "Adventure Ubud", "Wellness Ubud". The city is redundant - the user is already in the Ubud section.
  • Activity list only: "ATV + Rafting + Swing". Scannable but no vibe, no editorial hand.
  • Editorial title + activity sub-line. Two-line option: a magazine-style headline on top, the activity list as a small caption below. Captures vibe and facts.

The editorial pattern

Each alternative gets a short title that reads like a Condé Nast Traveller spread, and a one-line activity caption underneath.

A naming style guide for the team. Titles should be 2-5 words, written like article headlines. Good: "Up before dawn", "Pulse of the jungle", "Hidden rice fields, hidden beach". Avoid: brand jargon, exclamation marks, generic vibes ("Fun Day!"). The activity caption does the heavy lifting on what's actually in the day.
Question 3

3Sort alternatives by pace or by theme?

Two ways to organise the alternatives:

  • By pace (Slower / Balanced / More active). Matches our existing Pace metric. Solves the most common complaint.
  • By theme (Culture / Nature / Wellness / Adventure). Matches identity, the strongest emotional driver.

Recommendation: theme is the main axis. Pace is shown as a small label on each option.

This way users get the identity hook (theme) plus the pace info they need to compare. Best of both.

Also: variants that don't fit a city are simply skipped. No "Beach Ubud" because Ubud has no beach. No "Temple Seminyak" because Seminyak isn't a temple destination.

The flow:

Question 5

5Reordering days, with rules

You confirmed reordering is in scope, but only safely. Here's the rule:

The rule. A day can be reordered only with another day in the same city or region. Days that involve a transfer between regions cannot move.

Why this rule works

Every day on the itinerary is one of two kinds. The system already knows which is which.

Day type
Start / end
Reorderable?
Stay day
Starts and ends in the same city. No hotel change.
✓ Yes, with other stay days in the same city.
Transfer day
Starts in city A, ends in city B. Hotel change happens this day.
✗ No. Moving it would break hotel nights, transfers, and possibly flights.

How it shows up in the UI

  • On a stay day, the small "Change day" pill is joined by a tiny drag handle (six-dot icon) on the left of the day title. Long-press to pick up, drag to a new position within the same city block.
  • On a transfer day, the drag handle is absent and the day title shows a small "Transfer" tag. Tapping the tag shows a short explanation: "This day moves you from Ubud to Seminyak. It can't be shuffled."
  • While dragging, only the valid drop zones (same-city stay days) highlight. Invalid zones stay dimmed. The user can't accidentally drop somewhere that breaks the trip.

Question 6 (the killer feature)

6Swap while watching the video

When the user is in the full-screen video player, they've just experienced the day's vibe through actual footage. This is the highest-quality decision moment. They know exactly what they're getting and what they'd be giving up.

You ruled out bottom-center placement (rightly - that real estate is for captions). Two acceptable positions remain, both borrowed from Instagram Reels.

Two placement options, both Instagram-native

Option A · Right action rail

A small circular icon button on the right edge of the screen, vertically aligned with Like / Comment / Share / Save. Refresh icon, no label until tapped.

  • Pixel-perfect match with Instagram Reels, TikTok, Reels Shopping. Users find it instantly.
  • Stays out of the way of captions and the title.
  • Icon-only is slightly less discoverable than a labelled chip. We can show a one-time tooltip on first viewer open.

Putting it all together

  1. Replace the loud pink "Change day plan" link with a small grey "Change day" pill on each day row.
  2. Add the same pill on the day detail page header, alongside Photos and Videos.
  3. In the video viewer: add a refresh icon to the right action rail (Instagram-style), backed up by a labelled "Change day" chip next to the title for first-time discoverability.
  4. Tap any of these = popup slides up. Current day pinned, then 2-3 alternatives named editorially ("Up before dawn", "Pulse of the jungle", "Hidden rice fields, hidden beach"), each with an activity caption, pace pill, and price delta.
  5. If a swap would create 3 active days in a row, show an amber warning on the option before select.
  6. Picking is instant. Toast says "Swapped to X. Days 1-3 now active. [Rebalance] [Undo]".
  7. Footer price updates with a small animation.
  8. Drag handles on stay days enable reordering within the same city. Transfer days show a "Transfer" tag and a faded handle.
